1. Fill in the blanks:
(i) The solution used in a Voltaic cell is called ______.
Answer: electrolyte

(ii) A current carrying coil behaves like a ______.
Answer: magnet
2. Choose the correct option:
(i) Dry cells are less portable compared to Voltaic cells. (True/False)
Answer: False
Explanation: Dry cells are more portable than Voltaic cells because they do not contain liquid electrolyte and are sealed units.

(ii) A coil becomes an electromagnet only when electric current flows through it. (True/False)
Answer: True
Explanation: An electromagnet works only when current passes through the coil. Without current, it loses its magnetic properties.

(iii) An electromagnet, using a single cell, attracts more iron paper clips than the same electromagnet with a battery of 2 cells. (True/False)
Answer: False
Explanation: More cells provide more current, making the electromagnet stronger and able to attract more iron clips.
3. An electric current flows through a nichrome wire for a short time. (i) The wire becomes warm. (ii) A magnetic compass placed below the wire is deflected. Choose the correct option:

(a) Only (i) is correct
(b) Only (ii) is correct
(c) Both (i) and (ii) are correct
(d) Both (i) and (ii) are not correct

Answer: (c) Both (i) and (ii) are correct

Explanation: When current flows through nichrome wire, it produces both heating effect (wire becomes warm) and magnetic effect (compass needle deflects due to magnetic field around current-carrying conductor).

5. Nichrome wire is commonly used in electrical heating devices because it:

(i) is a good conductor of electricity
(ii) generates more heat for a given current
(iii) is cheaper than copper
(iv) is an insulator of electricity

Answer: (ii) generates more heat for a given current

Explanation: Nichrome has high resistance, so when current passes through it, more electrical energy is converted to heat energy. This makes it ideal for heating devices like electric heaters, toasters, and irons.
