Introduction to Pressure

Megha and Pawan's Story

Megha and Pawan are going on a picnic with identical bags. Pawan complains that his bag hurts his shoulders, while Megha feels comfortable. Why?

The difference is in their bag straps! Pawan's bag has narrow straps, while Megha's has broad straps.


Definition: Pressure is force spread over an area. It tells us how concentrated a force is.
Formula: Pressure = Force √∑ Area

Why Broad Straps Feel Better

Both bags have the same weight (force), but:

  • Narrow straps ‚Üí Force concentrated on small area ‚Üí High pressure ‚Üí Hurts shoulders
  • Broad straps ‚Üí Force spread over large area ‚Üí Low pressure ‚Üí Comfortable

Pressure in Liquids

Liquid Pressure: The pressure exerted by a liquid on the walls and bottom of its container is called liquid pressure. It depends on the height (or depth) of the liquid column, the density of the liquid, and the acceleration due to gravity, but it does not depend on the total amount (volume) of liquid present.

Why We're Not Crushed

Despite enormous atmospheric pressure, we don't get crushed because:

  • Pressure inside our bodies equals atmospheric pressure
  • This balance is maintained by fluids and gases in our tissues
  • Our bodies are adapted to function at this pressure

Wind Formation

Wind Definition: The movement of air from regions of high pressure to regions of low pressure. The greater the pressure difference, the faster the wind speed.

Pressure Differences Create Wind

Balloon Experiment

Demonstrating Pressure Equalization

When connected, air moves from inflated balloon (high pressure) to deflated balloon (low pressure) until pressures equalize. This demonstrates the fundamental principle behind wind formation.

High-Speed Winds and Reduced Pressure

Bernoulli's Principle: When the speed of a fluid (including air) increases, its pressure decreases. This explains why roofs can be blown off during storms.

Balloon Demonstration

Experiment

Two balloons hung close together move toward each other when air is blown between them.

This happens because blowing creates low pressure between balloons.

Higher pressure on outer sides pushes balloons together.

Application to Storms

High-speed winds over roofs create low pressure above them.

Higher pressure inside buildings can push roofs upward.

Keeping windows open reduces pressure difference.

Protection During Storms

Safety Measures

To prevent roof damage during high-speed winds:

  • Keep doors and windows open to equalize pressure
  • This allows wind to flow through rather than over the building
  • Reduces pressure difference between inside and outside

Lightning Formation

Charge Separation in Clouds

Strong upward and downward winds cause rubbing between:

  • Water droplets and ice particles
  • This creates static electric charges
  • Positive charges gather at top of clouds
  • Negative charges accumulate at bottom
  • Ground becomes positively charged

Lightning and Thunder

Process: Sudden discharge between opposite charges

Types: Within cloud, between clouds, or cloud to ground

Short Answer
6. Explain why dams are constructed with broader bases.
Answer:
Dams are constructed with broader bases because:
1. Water pressure increases with depth
2. The base of the dam experiences the highest water pressure
3. A broader base provides more area to withstand this pressure
4. This design prevents the dam from collapsing under the immense force of the water
5. The broader base also provides stability to the structure
Short Answer
7. Describe how lightning is formed.
Answer:
Lightning is formed through the following process:
1. Strong upward and downward winds in clouds cause rubbing between water droplets and ice particles
2. This friction creates static electric charges
3. Positive charges accumulate at the top of the cloud
4. Negative charges gather at the bottom of the cloud
5. The ground below becomes positively charged
6. When the charge difference becomes too great, the insulating property of air breaks down
7. A sudden discharge occurs as lightning, which can be within clouds, between clouds, or between cloud and ground
Long Answer
8. Explain the process of cyclone formation and its destructive effects.
Answer:
Cyclone Formation Process:
1. Forms over warm ocean waters (‚â•26.5¬∞C)
