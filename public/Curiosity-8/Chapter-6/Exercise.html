Pressure, Winds, Storms, and Cyclones

Curiosity Book - Exercises

1. Choose the correct statement.
(i) Look at Fig. 6.21 carefully. Vessel R is filled with water. When pouring of water is stopped, the level of water will be:

(a) the highest in vessel P
(b) the highest in vessel Q
(c) the highest in vessel R
(d) equal in all three vessels

Answer: (d) equal in all three vessels

Explanation: According to Pascal's law and the principle of communicating vessels, when vessels are connected at the bottom, water level becomes equal in all vessels regardless of their shape or size.


(ii) A rubber sucker (M) is pressed on a flat smooth surface and an identical sucker (N) is pressed on a rough surface:

(a) Both M and N will stick to their surfaces
(b) Both M and N will not stick to their surfaces
(c) M will stick but N will not stick
(d) M will not stick but N will stick

Answer: (c) M will stick but N will not stick

Explanation: Rubber sucker works due to atmospheric pressure. On smooth surface, sucker creates vacuum and atmospheric pressure holds it. On rough surface, air can enter through gaps, so no vacuum is created and sucker won't stick.


(iii) A water tank is placed on the roof of a building at a height 'H'. To get water with more pressure on the ground floor, one has to:

(a) increase the height 'H' at which the tank is placed
(b) decrease the height 'H' at which the tank is placed
(c) replace the tank with another tank of the same height that can hold more water
(d) replace the tank with another tank of the same height that can hold less water

Answer: (a) increase the height 'H' at which the tank is placed

Explanation: Water pressure increases with height. Pressure = ρgh, where h is height. Greater height means more pressure at ground level.


(iv) Two vessels, A and B, contain water up to the same level as shown in Fig. 6.22. PA and PB are the pressures at the bottom of the vessels. FA and FB are the forces exerted by the water at the bottom of the vessels A and B.

(a) PA = PB, FA = FB
(b) PA = PB, FA < FB
(c) PA < PB, FA = FB
(d) PA > PB, FA > FB

Answer: (b) PA = PB, FA < FB

Explanation:

  • The pressure at the bottom of a liquid column depends only on the height of the liquid, not on the shape of the vessel.
  • Since the water levels in A and B are the same, PA = PB.
  • The force exerted is given by: F = P × Area.
  • Vessel B has a larger base area than vessel A.
  • Therefore, FB > FA.

2. State whether the following statements are True [T] or False [F].
(i) Air flows from a region of higher pressure to a region of lower pressure.
Answer: True
Explanation: Air always moves from high pressure area to low pressure area. This movement creates wind.

(ii) Liquids exert pressure only at the bottom of a container.
Answer: False
Explanation: Liquids exert pressure in all directions - at the bottom, sides, and even upward direction.

(iii) Weather is stormy at the eye of a cyclone.
Answer: False
Explanation: The eye of a cyclone is calm and peaceful with clear skies. The stormy weather occurs around the eye wall.

(iv) During a thunderstorm, it is safer to be in a car.
Answer: True
Explanation: Cars act as Faraday cages. The metal body conducts electricity around the passengers and into the ground, providing protection from lightning.
3. Fig. 6.23a shows a boy lying horizontally, and Fig. 6.23b shows the boy standing vertically on a loose sand bed. In which case does the boy sink more in sand? Give reasons.
Answer: The boy sinks more when standing vertically (Fig. 6.23b).
Reasons:
• Pressure = Force/Area
• When lying horizontally, boy's weight is distributed over large body surface area
• When standing vertically, same weight is distributed over small area of feet
• Small area means high pressure on sand
• High pressure causes more sinking in loose sand
• This is why we use snow shoes or lie flat on thin ice to avoid sinking
4. An elephant stands on four feet. If the area covered by one foot is 0.25 m², calculate the pressure exerted by the elephant on the ground if its weight is 20000 N.
Given:
• Area covered by one foot = 0.25 m²
• Number of feet = 4
• Weight of elephant = 20000 N

To find: Pressure exerted by elephant

Solution:
Total area covered by all four feet = 4 × 0.25 = 1.0 m²

Pressure = Force/Area
Pressure = 20000 N / 1.0 m²
Pressure = 20000 N/m² = 20000 Pa

Answer: The pressure exerted by elephant = 20000 Pa or 20 kPa
5. There are two boats, A and B. Boat A has a base area of 7 m², and 5 persons are seated in it. Boat B has a base area of 3.5 m², and 3 persons are seating in it. If each person has a weight of 700 N, find out which boat will experience more pressure on its base and by how much?
Given:
• Boat A: Base area = 7 m², Persons = 5
• Boat B: Base area = 3.5 m², Persons = 3
• Weight of each person = 700 N

Solution:
For Boat A:
Total weight = 5 × 700 = 3500 N
Pressure on A = 3500 N / 7 m² = 500 N/m²

For Boat B:
Total weight = 3 × 700 = 2100 N
Pressure on B = 2100 N / 3.5 m² = 600 N/m²

Comparison:
Boat B experiences more pressure
Difference = 600 - 500 = 100 N/m²

Answer: Boat B will experience more pressure by 100 N/m² (or 100 Pa)
6. Would lightning occur if air and clouds were good conductors of electricity? Give reasons for your answer.
Answer: No, lightning would not occur if air and clouds were good conductors of electricity.
Reasons:
• Lightning occurs due to buildup of electric charges in clouds
• Air and water droplets in clouds are poor conductors (insulators)
• This allows electric charges to accumulate and build up
• When charge buildup becomes very high, it overcomes air resistance
• If air and clouds were good conductors, charges would flow continuously
• No charge accumulation means no sudden discharge
• Therefore, no lightning would occur
• Good conductors allow smooth flow of electricity without sparks
7. What will happen to the two identical balloons A and B as shown in Fig. 6.24 when water is filled into the bottle up to a certain height. Will both the balloons bulge? If yes, will they bulge equally? Explain your answer.
Answer: Both balloons will bulge equally.
Explanation:
• When water is poured into the bottle, it exerts pressure on the walls and balloons attached.
• Liquid pressure at a given depth is the same in all directions (P = ρgh).
• Since both balloons are at the same height, they experience equal pressure from the water.
• Therefore, both balloons bulge equally, showing that liquid pressure at the same depth is equal in all directions.
8. Explain how a storm becomes a cyclone.
Formation of Cyclone from Storm:

Step 1 - Initial Conditions:
• Storm begins with temperature difference between land and sea
• Warm, moist air rises creating low pressure area

Step 2 - Air Movement:
• Cool air from high pressure area rushes to fill low pressure area
• Due to Earth's rotation, moving air gets deflected (Coriolis effect)

Step 3 - Rotation Begins:
• Air starts rotating around the low pressure center
• Rising warm air releases heat energy, strengthening the system

Step 4 - Cyclone Formation:
• Wind speed increases (above 74 km/h becomes cyclone)
• Eye forms at center with calm conditions
• Spiral bands of clouds and strong winds develop around eye
• System becomes self-sustaining and can travel great distances
9. Fig. 6.25 shows trees along the sea coast in a summer afternoon. Identify which side is land— A or B. Explain your answer.
Answer: Side A is land and side B is sea.
Explanation:
• In summer afternoon, land gets heated faster than sea
• Hot air over land rises creating low pressure
• Cool air over sea creates high pressure
• Wind blows from sea (high pressure) to land (low pressure)
• This creates sea breeze flowing from B to A
• Trees bend in direction of wind flow
• Since trees are bending from B towards A, wind is blowing from sea (B) to land (A)
• Therefore, A is land and B is sea
• This is called sea breeze which occurs during daytime
10. Describe an activity to show that air flows from a region of high pressure to a region of low pressure.
Activity: The Two-Balloon Connection

Materials:
  • Two identical balloons
  • One drinking straw
  • Rubber bands or thread
Procedure:
  1. Attach the straw to the mouth of an uninflated balloon (A) with a rubber band.
  2. Inflate the second balloon (B), pinch it shut, and attach the other end of the straw to its mouth.
  3. Make sure both connections are airtight.
  4. Release the pinched end of the inflated balloon (B).
Observation:

Air flows from the inflated balloon (B) to the uninflated balloon (A), causing balloon A to inflate and balloon B to shrink. The flow continues until a pressure balance is reached.


Conclusion:

The stretched rubber of the inflated balloon creates high pressure inside it. The uninflated balloon is at a lower atmospheric pressure. Air moves through the straw from the region of high pressure (balloon B) to the region of low pressure (balloon A), demonstrating the fundamental principle of airflow.

11. What is a thunderstorm? Explain the process of its formation.
A thunderstorm is a violent local weather condition with heavy rain, strong winds, lightning, and thunder.

Formation Process:

1. Initial Stage:
• Strong heating of land surface creates rising hot air
• Water vapor in hot air condenses at high altitude forming clouds
• Cumulonimbus clouds develop rapidly

2. Charge Separation:
• Ice particles and water droplets in clouds collide
• Friction between particles creates electric charges
• Positive charges accumulate at top, negative at bottom

3. Lightning Formation:
• When charge difference becomes very large
• Electric discharge occurs between clouds or cloud and ground
• This creates lightning flash

4. Thunder Formation:
• Lightning heats air to very high temperature instantly
• Hot air expands rapidly creating sound waves
• This produces thunder sound
• Thunder is heard after lightning because sound travels slower than light
12. Explain the process that causes lightning.
Process of Lightning Formation:

Step 1 - Cloud Formation:
• Rising warm air creates cumulonimbus clouds
• These clouds contain water droplets and ice particles

Step 2 - Charge Building:
• Water droplets and ice particles move up and down in cloud
• Collision and friction between particles creates electric charges
• Lighter positive charges move to cloud top
• Heavier negative charges stay at cloud bottom

Step 3 - Electric Field:
• Large potential difference develops between cloud regions
• Electric field becomes very strong
• Air acts as insulator but breaks down under high voltage

Step 4 - Lightning Discharge:
• When electric field exceeds breakdown limit of air
• Sudden discharge occurs creating lightning bolt
• Current can be between clouds or cloud to ground
• Temperature of lightning channel reaches 30,000°C
• This extreme heat creates the bright flash we see
13. Explain why holes are made in banners and hoardings.
Answer: Holes are made in banners and hoardings to reduce wind pressure and prevent them from being torn or blown away.
Scientific Explanation:
• When wind hits a solid banner, it creates high pressure on one side
• This pressure difference can tear or topple the banner
• Holes allow wind to pass through, reducing pressure buildup
• Air flows through holes instead of pushing against entire surface
• This reduces the net force acting on the banner
• Similar principle used in designing buildings in cyclone-prone areas
• Reduces wind resistance and makes structures more stable

Additional Benefits:
• Prevents banners from acting like sails
• Reduces noise caused by flapping in wind
• Makes installation safer and more economical
