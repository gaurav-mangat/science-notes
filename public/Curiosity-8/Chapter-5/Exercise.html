2. State whether the following statements are True or False.
(i) A force is always required to change the speed of motion of an object.
Answer: True
Explanation: According to Newton's first law, an object continues in its state of motion unless acted upon by an external force. To change speed (accelerate or decelerate), a force is needed.

(ii) Due to friction, the speed of the ball rolling on a flat ground increases.
Answer: False
Explanation: Friction always opposes motion. When a ball rolls on flat ground, friction acts opposite to its motion, causing it to slow down, not speed up.

(iii) There is no force between two charged objects placed at a small distance apart.
Answer: False
Explanation: Charged objects exert electrostatic force on each other. Like charges repel and unlike charges attract, even when placed at a distance.
3. Two balloons rubbed with a woollen cloth are brought near each other. What would happen and why?
Answer: The two balloons will repel each other and move away from each other.
Explanation:
• When balloons are rubbed with woollen cloth, they acquire electric charge (electrons transfer from wool to balloon)
• Both balloons get the same type of charge (negative charge)
• Like charges always repel each other
• Therefore, the balloons push each other away due to electrostatic force
• This is an example of non-contact force acting at a distance
4. When you drop a coin in a glass of water, it sinks, but when you place a bigger wooden block in water, it floats. Explain.
Answer:

Coin sinks because:
• The coin is made of metal (usually copper or alloy) which is denser than water
• Weight of coin is greater than the buoyant force (upward force) exerted by water
• Gravitational force (downward) is stronger than buoyant force (upward)

Wooden block floats because:
• Wood is less dense than water
• Buoyant force exerted by water is greater than the weight of wooden block
• Upward buoyant force balances the downward gravitational force
• According to Archimedes' principle, the wooden block displaces water equal to its weight
5. If a ball is thrown upwards, it slows down, stops momentarily, and then falls back to the ground. Name the forces acting on the ball and specify their directions.
(i) During its upward motion
Forces acting:
• Gravitational force - acts downward (towards Earth)
• Air resistance - acts downward (opposite to motion)
Both forces oppose the upward motion, causing the ball to slow down.
(ii) During its downward motion
Forces acting:
• Gravitational force - acts downward (same direction as motion)
• Air resistance - acts upward (opposite to motion)
Net force is downward, causing the ball to accelerate towards ground.
(iii) At its topmost position
Forces acting:
• Gravitational force - acts downward
• At the highest point, velocity is zero but acceleration due to gravity continues to act downward, causing the ball to change direction and fall back.
6. A ball is released from the point P and moves along an inclined plane and then along a horizontal surface as shown in Fig. 5.16. It comes to stop at the point A on the horizontal surface. Think of a way so that when the ball is released from the same point P, it stops (i) before the point A (ii) after crossing the point A.
(i) To make the ball stop before point A:
Increase friction on the horizontal surface by:
• Making the surface rougher (use sandpaper or rough cloth)
• Sprinkling sand or powder on the surface
• Using a surface material with higher friction coefficient
• Adding obstacles or barriers that increase resistance
(ii) To make the ball stop after crossing point A:
Decrease friction on the horizontal surface by:
• Making the surface smoother (polish or use glass surface)
• Applying oil, soap, or lubricant to reduce friction
• Using a smoother material for the surface
• Ensuring the surface is clean and free from dust
7. Why do we sometimes slip on smooth surfaces like ice or polished floors? Explain.
Answer: We slip on smooth surfaces because there is very little friction between our shoes and the surface.
Detailed Explanation:
• Friction helps us walk by providing grip between our feet and ground
• On rough surfaces, friction is high, giving us better grip and stability
• On smooth surfaces like ice or polished floors, friction is very low
• Without enough friction, our feet cannot get proper grip
• When we try to walk, our feet slide instead of getting traction
• This causes us to lose balance and slip
• Ice is especially slippery because it melts slightly under pressure, creating a thin water layer
8. Is any force being applied to an object in a non-uniform motion?
Answer: Yes, a force is always being applied to an object in non-uniform motion.
Explanation:
• Non-uniform motion means the object is accelerating (changing speed or direction)
• According to Newton's first law, an object continues in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external force
• If motion is non-uniform (accelerated), there must be an unbalanced force acting on it
Examples:
  - A falling ball (gravitational force causes acceleration)
  - A car speeding up (engine force exceeds friction)
  - A ball rolling and slowing down (friction force opposes motion)
9. The weight of an object on the Moon becomes one-sixth of its weight on the Earth. What causes this change? Does the mass of the object also become one-sixth of its mass on Earth?
Answer:

What causes the change in weight:
• Weight depends on gravitational force (Weight = mass × acceleration due to gravity)
• Moon's gravitational acceleration is about 1/6th of Earth's gravity
• Earth's gravity = 9.8 m/s², Moon's gravity = 1.6 m/s²
• Since gravitational pull is weaker on Moon, weight becomes 1/6th

Does mass change?
• No, mass does NOT change
• Mass is the amount of matter in an object
• Mass remains constant everywhere in the universe
• Only weight changes with gravitational field strength
• An object with 60 kg mass on Earth will still have 60 kg mass on Moon
• But its weight will change from 588 N on Earth to 96 N on Moon
10. Three objects 1, 2, and 3 of the same size and shape but made of different materials are placed in the water. They dip to different depths as shown in Fig. 5.17. If the weights of the three objects 1, 2, and 3 are w₁, w₂, and w₃ respectively, then:

Options:
(i) w₁ = w₂ = w₃
(ii) w₁ > w₂ > w₃
(iii) w₁ < w₂ < w₃
(iv) w₂ > w₁ > w₃

Answer: (ii) w₁ > w₂ > w₃
Explanation:
• All objects are floating, so they are less dense than water
• According to Archimedes' principle, the submerged volume is proportional to the weight
• Object 1 is most submerged, so it has the highest weight
• Object 3 is least submerged, so it has the lowest weight
• Since all objects have the same size and shape, the one that dips more has higher density and weight
• Therefore: w₁ > w₂ > w₃
